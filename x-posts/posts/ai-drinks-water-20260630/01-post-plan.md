## Format Decision
SINGLE — the idea has one sharp payload: AI's resource cost is real but hidden from users. That fits in ≤280 chars without gutting it. Long-form nuance would blunt the punch; compression is the point on X.

## Core Insight
Every AI query consumes real electricity and drinkable fresh water — that cost exists, it's just been moved off the user's invoice and onto a ledger they never see.

## Target Audience
Developers, builders, and tech-adjacent professionals who use AI tools daily and have absorbed the "AI is getting cheaper / free tier" narrative without questioning what cheaper means for whom.

## Structure
SINGLE — one beat: the hidden cost was moved, not eliminated. Open with a claim that breaks the "free AI" frame. Land on the reveal. Close with a question that invites reflection.

## CTA / Reply hook
A question that's easy to answer with a real opinion: "What do you think a query is actually worth?" or "Have you thought about what your queries actually cost?" — invites genuine replies without being bait. No link for this idea; nothing to push to a reply.

## Tone
Contrarian / punchy — sarcastic in a dry way, not comedic. The rawness of the water scarcity angle should land as a factual gut-punch, not a hot take.

## Hashtags
None — the topic speaks for itself; hashtags would dilute the punchy delivery.

## Image Brief
Key contrast pairs the image should carry:
  - "Your bill" : $0.00
  - "Nature's invoice" : gallons of drinkable water
  - "The cost" : didn't disappear / was moved
  - "Free tier" : pricing decision / not physics
Tagline: "The cost didn't disappear. It moved."

## Angle Assignments

- **Angle 1 — Viral / reach play:** Open with the literal, arresting claim that an AI query "drank" real water. Short, declarative lines. Close on the "$0 billed ≠ $0 cost" contrast. Engineered for bookmarks — the kind of tweet people screenshot and send to friends with "did you know this??".

- **Angle 2 — Story / build-in-public:** A first-person pause mid-session — the moment of realizing the query you're about to ask costs more than you assumed. Relatable, specific, slightly uncomfortable. Targets replies through recognition ("same thing hit me").

- **Angle 3 — Contrarian / debate:** Quote the commonly held belief ("AI is getting cheaper") and immediately expose it as a half-truth. Close with the sharpest quotable line in the set: "Free tier is a pricing decision. Not a physics one." Targets quote-tweets and civil disagreement.
