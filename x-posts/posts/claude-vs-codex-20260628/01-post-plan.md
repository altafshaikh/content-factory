## Format Decision
SINGLE — The whole payload is one sharp contrast ("one tool opened a browser and tested my app like a user; the other needed three more prompts"). It lands in <280 chars without gutting it. A thread would dilute a punch that works best as a single quotable hit. Considered THREAD (context → contrast → nuance) but the context and nuance are support, not three co-equal sections — they belong in the tweet's middle line and an optional reply, not separate tweets.

## Core Insight
For frontend work right now, one AI tool acts on your intention autonomously (opens the browser, clicks through the flow, verifies it) while the other writes clean code but needs repeated hand-holding to understand what you actually meant.

## Target Audience
Developers and AI engineers building real apps with AI coding tools — people who've felt the gap between "generates code" and "understands intent and acts on it."

## Structure
SINGLE → land this one beat: same frontend task, both wrote clean code, but only one acted on the intention end-to-end without re-prompting. Close on a question that invites people to compare their own experience.

## CTA / Reply hook
Primary CTA = a question: which AI they use for frontend and how much re-prompting it costs them. No link exists in the source idea (it said "link in comments" but provided none), so no link in a reply. Use the reply slot for the "right tool, right job" nuance from the P.S. — it keeps the main tweet clean and pre-empts the "Claude is bad" misread.

## Tone
punchy / observational — first-person field note, not a benchmark or a hot take.
