# X (Twitter) Playbook — rules of the game

This is the X channel's substitute for a performance feedback loop. Free account = no analytics, so instead of *learning* what works from data, we *encode* what's already known about how X rewards and punishes content.

**Every run reads this file.** The Strategist reads it before planning; the Copywriters follow it while writing; the Editor checks against it. It's the craft baseline that keeps us from making naive mistakes.

Treat these as defaults, not laws — but break one only with a stated reason.

---

## 1. The first line is the whole game

X shows ~1 line before "Show more" and before the scroll. The first ~7 words decide whether the tweet is read at all.

- **Open on the payload.** Lead with the claim, the number, the tension, or the concrete moment — not setup.
- **No throat-clearing.** Kill "I've been thinking about…", "Here's a thread on…", "Let me tell you…", "Unpopular opinion:".
- **No "🧵" / "a thread" / "1/n as the hook."** The hook IS the first tweet; numbering is fine but it doesn't replace a hook.
- A good hook does one of: makes a specific claim, drops a surprising number, opens a curiosity gap, or names a real moment ("Spent 3 hours debugging what turned out to be a typo").
- If you wouldn't stop scrolling for your own first line, rewrite it.

---

## 2. Single tweet vs thread

- **Default to a single tweet.** One sharp idea in ≤280 chars beats a padded thread every time.
- **Thread only when the idea genuinely has ≥3 distinct sections** — sequential steps, a multi-part argument, or a before→after→lesson arc that loses meaning if compressed.
- A thread must earn every tweet. 3–7 tweets, no filler. If two tweets can merge, merge them.
- **Tweet 1 of a thread must stand alone** — it works as a tweet even if no one expands the thread. It carries the hook AND a reason to keep reading.
- The **last tweet lands the point and invites a reply** (a question or a take), never a limp "that's it, follow me for more."

---

## 3. Character economy (280 hard limit)

- Every tweet ≤280 characters. No exceptions. Count them.
- Cut every word that isn't load-bearing. "I think that maybe" → delete. Adverbs → usually delete.
- Prefer short, declarative sentences. Periods over commas. One idea per line.
- White space is a feature — line breaks make a tweet scannable and increase dwell.
- Numbers as numerals (3, not three). Contractions are fine — they read human.

---

## 4. What suppresses reach (don't do these)

| Mistake | Why it hurts | Do instead |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **External link in the main tweet** | X downranks tweets that send people off-platform | Put the link in a **reply** to your own tweet |
| **Hashtags** | Read as spam, don't help reach in 2020s X | None by default; at most 1 real community tag |
| **Engagement bait** ("RT if…", "comment YES") | Algorithm demotes obvious bait | Earn engagement with a genuine question or strong take |
| **Editing/deleting right after posting** | Resets/penalizes early distribution | Get it right before posting |
| **@-mentioning accounts in the first tweet** | Can scope the tweet to the reply graph (lower reach) | Mention people later in the thread or in a reply |
| **Walls of text, no line breaks** | Low scannability, low dwell | Break into short lines |
| **Cramming 2+ ideas into one tweet** | Dilutes the hook, confuses the reader | One idea per tweet |

## 5. Engagement mechanics (how X actually distributes)

- **Replies are the strongest signal**, especially when the author replies back and a conversation forms. Write posts that are easy and inviting to reply to.
- **Bookmarks are a top quality signal** — "save-for-later" without public cost. Tactical / how-to / reference content earns bookmarks. Lean into genuinely useful posts.
- **Reposts and quote-tweets** are reach multipliers. A post worth re-sharing has a clear, quotable line.
- **The first ~30 minutes matter most.** Reply to early comments fast — it compounds distribution.
- Engagement hierarchy: **replies > quote-tweets > reposts > bookmarks > likes > impressions.** Optimize for the left side.
- **"Time spent" is the algorithm's primary optimization metric (2024).** Anything that makes people stop and read longer wins: a hook that earns the next line, scannable formatting, an image/video, a thread people finish. Write to hold attention, not just to be liked.
- **Questions reliably generate replies** — a single well-aimed question can pull hundreds of responses (the source guide cites one post with 668 replies). When the goal is conversation, end on a specific, easy-to-answer question.
- **Real-time / trending topics in the niche** get more discovery — when an idea connects to something current, say so.

---

## 6. CTA patterns that work (without being cringe)

- **Best CTA on X is a question** that invites a real answer ("What's the dumbest bug that cost you hours?").
- A strong **take stated as fact** also invites replies (people correct/agree).
- **Never** "DM me" for an internal/proprietary tool, and never beg for follows/RTs.
- If there's something to link, the close is: main tweet stands alone → link in the first reply ("Wrote it up here: <link>").

---

## 7. Voice (sound like Altaf, not a brand)

- Conversational, direct, specific. Concrete beats abstract every time ("cut my build from 30 min to 4" > "improved efficiency").
- Real > polished. A slightly rough true line beats a smooth generic one.
- No corporate jargon, no buzzword soup, no "leverage/synergy/unlock."
- No emojis unless explicitly asked.
- Strip company names, internal tool names, private details — keep the lesson, drop the specifics.
- Never invent stats, quotes, or claims not in the raw idea.

---

## 8. Images

- A single image stops the scroll — but it must **add**, not decorate.
- X dimensions: **Landscape 1600×900 (16:9)** in-stream default, or **Square 1080×1080 (1:1)** quote-card. No portrait.
- Best image use: one bold quotable line, a clean stat, or a before/after — not a busy infographic.

---

## 9. Rookie-mistake checklist (Editor runs this)

Before any tweet ships, confirm:

- [ ] Every tweet ≤ 280 characters
- [ ] First ~7 words are a real hook, not setup
- [ ] No link in the main / first tweet (links live in a reply)
- [ ] No hashtags (or exactly one justified community tag)
- [ ] One idea per tweet
- [ ] If a thread: tweet 1 stands alone; last tweet invites a reply
- [ ] CTA is a question or strong take — not "DM me" / "RT if"
- [ ] No emojis (unless asked), no jargon, no invented facts
- [ ] No company/internal-tool names
- [ ] Reads like a human talking, not a press release

---

## 10. Timing (general, not measured)

We can't measure our own best times (no analytics), so use sensible defaults: weekday mornings and around lunch in the author's timezone tend to perform; very late night underperforms unless the audience is global. This is guidance for *when Altaf posts*, not something the pipeline controls.

---

## 11. Why people follow — every post must do one job

People follow accounts for a reason. Each post should clearly deliver at least one of these (pick 1–2, don't try for all five):

| Reason | The post... | Maps to angle |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **TIL** ("today I learned") | teaches something concrete | Tactical / Story |
| **FIRED-UP** | motivates / energizes | Story / Viral |
| **LOL** | entertains, relatable | Viral / Story |
| **HMM** | makes them think, reframes | Contrarian / Viral |
| **FINALLY** | says what they couldn't articulate | Contrarian / Viral |

If a draft doesn't land on at least one, it's a forgettable post — send it back.

The post skeleton stays simple: **Hook → Value (teach / motivate / entertain / reframe) → CTA (a question or a reason to share).**

---

## 12. Growth habits — Altaf's manual actions (outside the pipeline)

These aren't things the generator produces — they're habits that compound reach. Surface them as reminders, don't try to automate them:

- **Post 1–2x daily** about the niche. Consistency beats volume.
- **Reply to bigger creators with genuine value** within the niche (find high-engagement posts with the `min_faves:500` search filter; turn on notifications for a few to be an early, useful first-responder). **Never self-promote in replies** — it gets you blocked.
- **Quote-tweet** strong posts and add a real perspective — borrows reach and shows thinking.
- **Refresh the pinned post every ~48 hours** — it re-broadcasts to followers.
- **Bookmark great posts to remix later** — a private swipe file of formats that worked.
- **Engage in DMs / build real relationships** — collaborations (podcasts, shout-outs) move the needle more than any single tweet.
- Avoid cringe growth-hacks; lead with authentic ideas.
