# Reel Plan — Your notes → slides in seconds

## Style & Format

Style: FACELESS — screen recording (iPhone) + voiceover. The demo IS the hook; no face needed. Showing the phone doing the work is more persuasive than explaining it.
Format: screen-demo
Length: 38s — long enough to show the problem → setup concept → workflow live → payoff; short enough to keep completion above 60%. The result appears at second 28 so the CTA lands before most drop-off.
Audio: original voiceover — the narration is the value here (explaining what's happening on screen). A vague trending track would undercut clarity. Voiceover also makes it evergreen.

---

## Core Insight

The gap between a rough idea and a finished presentation isn't a creativity problem — it's an activation-energy problem. One custom shortcut removes it.

---

## Target Viewer

A developer, builder, or tech-adjacent creator in their 20s-40s who constantly has ideas but almost never turns them into structured decks. Has an iPhone. Has used AI. Has a graveyard of unprocessed notes. Doesn't think of themselves as "a presenter" but knows they should get their ideas out more.

---

## Hook (0-2s)

On-screen text (bold, white on dark): **"Your notes → slides in seconds"**
Spoken/VO: "Your rough notes can become a structured slide deck before you even open your laptop."
Visual: iPhone Notes app open with visible messy text, then a fast cut to a clean slide outline appearing — the result shown first, then the "how."

---

## Beat Skeleton

1. **Hook (0-2s)** — Show the transformation promise. Notes app with messy text → slide outline flashes in. On-screen: "Your notes → slides in seconds."

2. **The problem (2-8s)** — "I had 30+ ideas sitting in my Notes app. Every time I tried to turn one into a slide deck, I'd see the messy text, feel the friction, and close the laptop. Not laziness — just a bad effort-to-value ratio." On-screen: "The problem: ideas die in Notes." Visual: scrolling through a Notes app packed with unstructured brain-dumps.

3. **The solution (8-18s)** — "So I built a custom iPhone shortcut. It does three things: takes any note as input, passes it to AI with my own slide structure rules already written in, and hands back a structured outline." On-screen: "Shortcut + AI + your own layout rules." Visual: iPhone Shortcuts app, shortcut card visible, then a prompt preview.

4. **Live demo (18-30s)** — "Open the note. Hit Share. Tap the shortcut. Done." On-screen: "Share → Shortcut → Done." Visual: Screen recording — Notes app → Share Sheet → shortcut triggers → result appears.

5. **The payoff (30-36s)** — "Every section mapped. Titles, key points, slide flow — exactly the layout I prefer. No blank Keynote staring back at me." On-screen: "Structured. Ready to build." Visual: Clean slide outline in the output, scrolling through a few sections.

6. **CTA (36-38s)** — "Save this to build yours. What's your biggest bottleneck between a rough idea and a finished presentation?" On-screen: "What's blocking you?" Visual: Final frame with shortcut icon.

Payoff/CTA — save this to build it; comment with your bottleneck (drives comment engagement + saves).
