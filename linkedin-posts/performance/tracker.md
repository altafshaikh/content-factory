### LinkedIn algorithm signals

- **Best comment rate proxy:** gen-ai-roadmap (7.69% total eng.) and ai-drinks-water (7.43%) — both likely had strong comment-to-impression ratios based on total eng. rate
- **Format breakdown:** text + image is dominant across all posts; one text-only (ai-drinks-water) performed strongly — not a disadvantage
- **Link-in-post tax evidence:** gen-ai-roadmap had an external link and still outlier — but do not generalize. Move all links to first comment.
- **Impressions vs. engagement rate:** routines (848 imp, 2.48%) shows reach without depth. The unmatched 7/2 post (742 imp, 2.16%) shows the same pattern. High impressions with low conversion suggests topic had algorithmic reach but hook failed to convert.
- **Hashtag pattern on top posts:** gen-ai-roadmap used `#GenAI #AIEngineering #SoftwareEngineering` — broad but topically tight. ai-drinks-water used `#AI #AIInfrastructure` — minimal. Both suggest fewer, more specific hashtags outperform generic clusters.

### Audience behavior patterns

- Practical skill-building and roadmap content drives the strongest engagement (gen-ai-roadmap top performer)
- Contrarian posts that name something the audience does but doesn't admit drive comments (ai-drinks-water)
- Topic-specific comparisons (A vs B developer tool) drive engagement because they're "taking sides" posts
- Abstract process posts (routines, "I built a system") get broad reach but shallow engagement — need stronger hooks

### Recommendations for Strategist

1. **Lead with a specific, uncomfortable truth** rather than a scene. "I wasn't learning Gen AI. I was consuming it." beats "Last week at 8:30 PM, my laptop was closed." The first is an indictment; the second is a scene.
2. **Contrarian angle is underused** — only one post so far. Test it on developer tool and AI agent topics, not just sustainability.
3. **Bold claim-first is inconsistent** — it works when the claim has two sides (comparison), not when it introduces a capability. Filter: "Would someone argue against this claim?" If no, it's not a bold claim — it's just a statement.
4. **External links kill reach** — move all URLs to first comment. gen-ai-roadmap beat it but that's the exception.
5. **High impressions with low eng. rate** is a warning sign, not a win — the routines post reached many but didn't convert. Prioritize hook sharpness over distribution.
6. **Verify unpublished posts:** superreps-learnings, build-your-first-agents-team, just-text, run-your-own-agent — none appeared in TOP 50. Either not published or zero engagement. Confirm status.
