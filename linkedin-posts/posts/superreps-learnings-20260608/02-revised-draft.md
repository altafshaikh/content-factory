One prompt will not build your app.

I learned this building a personal fitness tracker — AI at every step. Design references in. Code generated. Bugs fixed. Layouts iterated.

Six screens in, the UI looked clean.
Nothing worked.

And here is the thing: AI is fast at producing output. It is slow at producing *usefulness*.

That gap — between "this looks like an app" and "this works like an app" — is where all the real work lives. AI cannot own that gap for you.

I ended up refining screen by screen, flow by flow. Every transition. Every edge case. Every path a real user might take. There was no shortcut.

The agent could design components in isolation.
It could not stitch them into something a user picks up and understands in 10 seconds.

What I keep coming back to: AI accelerates the build. It does not replace thinking about how the build needs to *feel*.

You still have to hold the product vision. You still have to test the full flow — because the agent won't.

Building anything non-trivial with AI right now? Where did your first real wall appear — the UI, the logic, or the end-to-end flow?
