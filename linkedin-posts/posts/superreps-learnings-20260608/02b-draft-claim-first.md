One prompt will not build your app.

This is the thing people don't say loudly enough when they talk about AI-assisted development.

I've been building a personal fitness tracker — vibe coding it from scratch, using AI at every step. Designing the UI, generating the code, fixing the bugs, iterating on the layout. The whole thing.

And here is what I found:

AI is fast at producing output.
It is slow at producing *usefulness*.

Screens appeared quickly. The visual layer came together. Then I tried to wire everything up — and that's when it fell apart.

UI rendered. Features missing.
Features added. UX broken.
UX fixed on one screen. Another screen regressed.

The gap between "this looks like an app" and "this works like an app" is where all the real work lives. And AI cannot own that gap for you.

What I ended up doing: refining screen by screen. Flow by flow. Testing every transition, every edge case, every path a user might take. There was no shortcut.

This was not boilerplate. The agent could design components in isolation. It could not stitch them into something a real user would pick up and understand in 10 seconds.

The lesson I keep coming back to:

AI accelerates the build. It does not replace the thinking about how the build needs to *feel*.

You still have to hold the product vision. You still have to catch what the agent drops. You still have to test the full flow end to end — because the agent won't.

Building anything non-trivial with AI right now? Where did your first real wall appear?
