Two developers. Same AI tools. Completely different outcomes.

Developer A — strong fundamentals:
→ Knows what to build before asking AI
→ Can break down the problem
→ Catches wrong answers before shipping
→ Gets 10x faster

Developer B — weak fundamentals:
→ Lets AI decide what to build
→ Can't verify if the output is correct
→ Ships faster — and breaks things faster
→ Gets 10x confused

Same tools. Different foundations. Different results.

A few years ago, learning to code was enough.

Today, coding is the easy part.

The hard part is knowing what to build, how to debug, how to design systems, how to ask the right question.

And I see a lot of developers skipping that part.

Collecting tutorials, prompts, frameworks, the latest model.

But never building the judgment that makes any of those useful.

Tools will change. AI models will change. Frameworks will change.

The ability to think clearly and solve problems won't.

That's the skill that compounds for decades.

Are you using AI at your work everyday? How has your working style changed in the last 1-2 years?
