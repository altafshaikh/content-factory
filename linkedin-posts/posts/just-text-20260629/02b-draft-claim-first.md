AI is going to make half of developers dramatically faster — and the other half dramatically more confused.

The difference has nothing to do with which tools they use.

It has everything to do with their fundamentals.

A developer with strong foundations can use AI as a force multiplier.

They know what to build.
They know how to break down a problem.
They can debug when AI gives a wrong answer.
They can design systems that actually scale.

A developer with weak foundations uses AI as a crutch.

They trust outputs they can't verify.

They ship faster — toward the wrong thing.

I see this constantly.

Developers collecting courses, prompts, frameworks, the latest model release.

Spending hours on tools they barely understand.

While the fundamentals stay weak.

And in the AI era, weak fundamentals don't stay quiet.

They get amplified.

Tools will change. Frameworks will change. AI models will change.

The ability to think clearly and solve problems is the only skill that compounds for decades.

Build that first.
