Someone asked me last week: "Should I learn DSA or focus on AI tools?"

My answer took them by surprise.

A few years ago, learning to code was enough.

Today, coding is the easy part.

What actually separates developers now isn't which AI tool they know.

It's whether they can:
- Figure out what to build
- Break down a complex problem
- Debug when AI gives a wrong answer
- Design systems that actually scale
- Ask the right question in the first place

I see developers spending hours collecting courses, frameworks, and AI tools.

Their reading list is growing.

But their fundamentals are not.

And that's becoming dangerous.

Because AI is a multiplier.

It takes what you already have and amplifies it.

Strong fundamentals? You get 10x faster.

Weak fundamentals? You get 10x confused.

So my answer to "should I learn DSA or AI?" is the same answer I've been giving for years:

Don't chase tools.

Build strong foundations.

Tools will change. Frameworks will change. AI models will change.

The ability to think clearly and solve problems won't.

That's the skill that compounds for decades.

Are you using AI at your work everyday? How has your working style changed in the last 1-2 years?
