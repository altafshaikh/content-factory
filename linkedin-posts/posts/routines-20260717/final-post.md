## Final Post

GitHub Actions is not enough anymore.

Not because it's broken.

Because it can't think.

When a workflow hits an edge case it wasn't written for, it exits with an error code. You come back to a red build and start debugging manually. That's the ceiling of scripts: they follow rules, and rules have gaps.

Claude Code Routines works differently.

You define a task and a schedule. Claude runs it in the cloud, reasons through what it finds, and adapts when something unexpected happens. Not exits. Adapts.

That's the shift: from scripts that follow rules to agents that reason.

For a solo developer, this changes the math:

- PR reviews happening while you sleep
- Issue triage running overnight
- Nightly deploys executing without you there
- Morning standup prep ready before your first coffee

Stack these as routines and you've given yourself a 24/7 engineering team.

Until now, if you closed your laptop, your AI automation died.

That constraint is gone.

Claude Routines runs in the cloud on a schedule. Not on your machine. Not dependent on your terminal being open.

The question isn't whether to automate. It's what to automate first.

What would you automate first if your AI worked around the clock?
