Close your laptop.

Your AI is still running.

Until now, that wasn't possible. Every automation you built died the moment you closed the terminal. You had to be there. Manually trigger it. Babysit it through the whole run.

That changed with Claude Code Routines.

You define a task and a schedule. Claude runs it in the cloud, on its own, while you're asleep, on a call, or away from your desk.

What does that actually unlock?

Think about the work that piles up every evening:

- PR reviews that nobody gets to until morning
- Issues coming in overnight with no triage
- Deploys that should have run by 2 AM
- Standup prep eating the first 20 minutes of every day

Turn each one into a routine. Stack them: morning standup prep, midday deploy check, nightly issue triage.

That's a full-time DevOps engineer's output, running without you.

The deeper shift isn't the scheduling.

It's the reasoning.

Scripts follow rules. They break when an edge case they weren't written for shows up. You come back to a red build and start debugging manually.

Claude doesn't follow rules. It reasons through them. When a test fails unexpectedly, it investigates. It adapts.

For a solo developer or a small team, this might be the most leveraged thing you can set up right now.

What would you automate first if your AI worked around the clock?
