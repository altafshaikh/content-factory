Four steps to an AI routine that works while you sleep.

This is how Claude Code Routines actually works.

Step 1: Decide
What's the task that eats your time but follows a predictable pattern? PR reviews, issue triage, nightly deploys, standup prep. Pick one.

Step 2: Create
Set up the routine in Claude Code with a schedule. The agent runs in the cloud on that schedule. Not on your laptop. Not dependent on your terminal being open.

Step 3: Prompt
This is where most people lose the gains. A weak prompt fails silently. A strong one adapts.

The anatomy of a routine prompt that holds up:
- Role: what kind of agent is this?
- Task: what exactly should it do?
- Steps: in what order?
- Output: what should the result look like?
- Constraints: what should it never do?
- Error handling: what happens when something breaks?

Step 4: Connect
Hook it to Slack, GitHub, or whatever you actually use. An autonomous routine that reports nowhere is one you'll stop checking.

The stack that makes sense for most solo developers:
Morning: standup prep
Midday: deploy check
Nightly: issue triage

Three routines. One setup. Full-time DevOps output, running 24/7.
