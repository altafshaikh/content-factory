# Post Plan — non-deterministic-vs-deterministic

## Core Insight
AI agents are non-deterministic by nature, which means every automation you build on them requires ongoing instruction maintenance — a hidden cost that programmatic code never has.

## Target Audience
Mid-career software engineers and builder-types who are actively integrating AI agents into their workflows and starting to feel the friction of maintaining them over time.

## Structure
1. Establish the contrast: traditional code gives you the same output forever; AI agents don't
2. The compounding problem: agents drift — they skip instructions, introduce new mistakes on subsequent runs
3. The vendor dimension: AI companies degrade old models, breaking your automations without you changing a line
4. The honest builder reality: you can't "write it once and forget it" with agents — it's a living system
5. Open question: how do you solve this? What's your approach?

## CTA
What's your approach to this? Have you found a way to make AI automations more stable, or do you treat them as living systems?

## Tone Range
Conversational · builder-honest · slightly provocative — this challenges the narrative that "AI will automate everything" without nuance

## Hashtags
#AIEngineering #SoftwareDevelopment #BuildingWithAI #AgentDevelopment #DeveloperExperience

Stats/contrast pairs to carry:
  - "Same input → Same output" (traditional code) vs "Same input → ?" (AI agent)
  - "Write once. Run forever." vs "Write once. Maintain always."
  - "Zero variance" vs "Non-deterministic"
Tagline: "The hidden cost of AI agents"
Before/after framing: Traditional code vs. agent-based automation maintenance burden

- Implication for THIS post: Comparison/split-screen framing has an early edge — lead with the stark contrast (deterministic vs non-deterministic) rather than burying it in the story. Bold claim or split-screen open likely to outperform story-first here.

## Angle Assignments

**Angle A:** Story-first — open with the moment Altaf realized his working automation started behaving differently after a model update, let the insight about non-determinism emerge from experience.

**Angle B:** Bold claim-first — lead with the provocative claim that AI agents have a hidden maintenance tax that traditional code never has, then prove it.

**Angle C:** Comparison/split-screen-first — Decision rule 3 applies first: the idea is structurally a comparison (traditional programming vs AI agent-based automation). Bake the two sides into the opening line.
