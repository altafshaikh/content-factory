I had a voice memo and two pages of rough notes for a talk I wanted to give.

Both sat untouched for three weeks.

Not because the talk wasn't worth giving.

The real reason: going from raw notes to a structured slide deck felt like too much work for any given evening.

That's not a skills problem. That's a friction problem.

The brain avoids high-effort tasks. Converting rough thinking into structured slides is a high-effort task. So ideas pile up in notes and nothing gets built.

Here's how I closed that gap.

I wrote a custom prompt — my entire slide layout logic encoded once. Which sections to include, how to structure each slide, how to choose a title, what to leave out. Everything I've learned about building a good presentation, written down in one place.

Then I wired it to an iPhone shortcut.

The workflow: select a note from the Notes app, share it to the shortcut, receive a structured slide layout in seconds.

The shortcut passes my notes to an AI model along with that prompt. The model returns the layout. I refine from there.

I went from three weeks of procrastination to a working deck structure in under a minute.

The principle works beyond slides. Any task where you know what good output looks like — but the gap between raw input and that output feels too wide — is a candidate for this approach.

What's something in your workflow you've automated with AI to close a friction gap like this?
