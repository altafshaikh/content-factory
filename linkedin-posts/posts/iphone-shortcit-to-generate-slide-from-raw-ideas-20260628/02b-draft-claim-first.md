Your raw ideas aren't turning into slides because of a skills problem.

It's a friction problem.

The brain avoids high-effort tasks. Opening a blank presentation and converting rough notes into structured slides is a high-effort task.

So the ideas stay stuck in notes. Talks don't get built. Decks get delayed indefinitely.

Here's how I closed that gap.

I wrote a single custom prompt — my entire slide layout logic encoded once. Which points to include, how to structure each section, how to choose a title, what to leave out. Years of thinking about what makes a good presentation, written down in one place.

Then I wired it to an iPhone shortcut.

The workflow: select a note from the Notes app, share it to the shortcut, and receive a structured slide layout in seconds.

The shortcut passes my notes to an AI model along with that prompt. The model returns the layout. I refine from there.

I went from weeks of procrastination on a talk to a working deck structure in under a minute.

The principle works beyond slides. Any task where you know what good output looks like — but the gap between raw input and that output feels too wide — is a candidate for this same approach.
