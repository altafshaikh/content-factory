Here's how I turned my Notes app into a slide generator using a single iPhone shortcut.

Step 1: Write your custom prompt.

Not a generic one. Your layout — which sections to include, how to title each slide, how to structure the opening and close, what to cut. Everything you know about building a good presentation, written down once.

Step 2: Build the shortcut.

On iPhone, create a shortcut that takes input from the Notes app. Pass that input to an AI model along with your custom prompt.

Step 3: Use it.

Open any note — rough transcript, voice memo dump, bullet points from a meeting. Tap "Share" and select the shortcut. Receive a structured slide layout in seconds.

The shortcut doesn't do the thinking. You already did the thinking when you wrote the prompt. It just removes the blank-slide problem, which is the part that causes procrastination.

The deeper principle: the gap between raw notes and polished output usually isn't a knowledge gap. It's a setup cost.

When you encode your own standards into a reusable prompt and wire it to a shortcut, that setup cost drops to near zero.

This same pattern works for any output you produce repeatedly: meeting summaries, project briefs, documentation drafts.
