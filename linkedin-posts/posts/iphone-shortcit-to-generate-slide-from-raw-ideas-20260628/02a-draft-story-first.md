I had a voice memo and two pages of rough notes for a talk I wanted to give.

Both sat untouched for three weeks.

Not because the talk wasn't worth giving.

Because opening a blank slide deck felt like a wall.

This is the procrastination trap most builders don't name clearly.

It's not that the idea is bad. It's that the gap between raw thinking and structured output costs more cognitive energy than the brain wants to spend in that moment.

So the notes pile up.

I decided to close that gap.

I wrote a custom prompt — every layout preference I've developed over the years. Which sections to include. How to structure each slide. How to choose a title. What to cut. The entire logic of a good presentation, encoded once.

Then I built an iPhone shortcut that takes notes directly from my Notes app, runs them through an AI model with that prompt, and returns a structured slide layout.

Now the workflow is: open a note, share to the shortcut, receive a draft deck structure in under a minute.

The blank slide problem is gone.

The shortcut doesn't write the slides. It writes the first draft — enough to start, which was always the hard part.

There are likely tasks in your workflow with the same shape: you know what good output looks like, but the setup cost stops you every time.

What's something in your workflow you've automated with AI to close a friction gap like this?
