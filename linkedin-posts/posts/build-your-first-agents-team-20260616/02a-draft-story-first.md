The first time I wired two AI models together, something clicked.

I wasn't prompting anymore.
I was building a system.

One model reads an idea.
Another shapes it into a plan.
A third writes the output.

None of them "know" the goal.
Together, they achieve it.

That's the difference between a prompt and an agent system.

A prompt finishes a task.
An agent system solves a problem.

I've built two of these now — one that runs a content pipeline, one that automates a security workflow.
Both are just three agents, wired around a shared goal.

The architecture is simpler than you think.
You don't need to be an AI researcher.
You need to know how to map a problem to roles.

Role 1: Understands the input.
Role 2: Does the work.
Role 3: Reviews and passes it forward.

That's it.
