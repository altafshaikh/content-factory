Most engineers are using AI wrong.

Not because they're bad at prompting.
Because prompting is the wrong tool for solving problems.

Prompting finishes tasks.
Agent systems solve problems.

There's a difference — and it changes what you can build.

A single prompt: you ask, it answers, you move on.
An agent system: Model A reads, Model B plans, Model C executes — all chained around one goal.

The handoff is what makes it a system.

I've shipped two of these. One handles content from raw ideas to final output. One automates a security review workflow.
Both are three agents. Both solve a problem I couldn't automate with a single prompt.

Here's the part most people miss: you don't have to architect this from scratch.

You map a problem to three roles:
1. Understand the input
2. Do the work
3. Review and pass it forward

Any problem you have can fit that shape.
