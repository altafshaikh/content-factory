The first program every developer writes is Hello World.

Not because it does anything useful.
Because it proves the machine listens.

I still remember that feeling — typing those two words, hitting run, and seeing them printed back.
It sounds small. It wasn't.

Something similar happened to me recently.

I handed a PRD to a team of autonomous agents.
No code written by me. No guidance during execution. No handholding.

I just triggered them and stepped back.

They planned the work themselves.
They broke it into tasks.
They wrote the code.
They ran the tests.
They caught the issues and fixed them.
They marked it complete.

Four ticks. Each tick shipped a feature.

At the end, I had a fully working todo application.

Todo apps are the Hello World of learning something new.
When you want to test whether you understand a framework, a language, a paradigm — you build a todo app.

So the symmetry hit me.

My first real program: Hello World.
My first real agent team output: a working todo app.

Same feeling. Different era.

We talk a lot about AI agents in theory.
This was just a PRD going in, and a working app coming out.
No interventions. No output edits. Just the result.

That's the shift.

"Deploy your first agent team" is the new Hello World.
